I'm an AI safety researcher with a background in pure mathematics. I work on scalable oversight — currently studying AI debate protocols and how to make them closer to formal proof than rhetorical argument — and I train small models to build intuition about how learning dynamics change with scale.

My research is funded by Open Philanthropy through the Berkeley Existential Risk Initiative.

Research

  • Symbolic debate for scalable oversight. Redesigning AI debate so a proposer proves claims and a refuter searches for logical flaws, rather than two models arguing persuasively. Measuring how far current models are from this regime and whether the gap closes with scale.
  • Small-model training dynamics. Training models in the 1M–150M parameter range to study architecture-agnostic phenomena: grokking, memorization vs. generalization, and what transfers across tasks.

Projects

  • master-reach — a minimal PyTorch research harness for training and instrumenting small models. Flat training loop, no framework, full visibility into every step.
  • ai-debate — an experimental framework for AI debate as a scalable-oversight protocol: proposer/refuter roles, judge calibration, and metrics that compare debate against direct QA fairly.
